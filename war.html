The Kurukshetra War

The Kurukshetra War, the climax of the Mahabharata, was an 18-day battle fought on the plains of Kurukshetra between the Pandavas and the Kauravas. After years of enmity, culminating in the Pandavas’ exile and the failure of peace negotiations, the two sides gathered massive armies, with the Pandavas supported by allies like the Yadavas and the Kauravas backed by warriors like Bhishma and Drona.

On the first day, the armies clashed with great ferocity, following the rules of dharma in warfare. However, as the days progressed, the war became increasingly brutal. Key events included Bhishma’s fall on the tenth day, struck down by Arjuna with Shikhandi’s help; Drona’s death on the fifteenth day, deceived by Yudhishthira’s words; and Karna’s demise on the seventeenth day, killed by Arjuna while his chariot wheel was stuck. On the final day, Bhima defeated Duryodhana in a mace duel, fulfilling his vow to avenge Draupadi’s humiliation.

The war ended with the Pandavas’ victory, but at a great cost—millions perished, including most of the Kuru lineage. Yudhishthira became king, but the victory was bittersweet, leading to deep reflections on the nature of dharma and the cost of war, as captured in the Bhagavad Gita, spoken by Krishna to Arjuna before the battle began.

Reflecting on this epic conflict, the Kurukshetra War teaches us about the complexities of duty, the inevitability of conflict, and the pursuit of justice.
